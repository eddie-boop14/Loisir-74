Col des Aravis

The Col des Aravis links La Clusaz, in Haute-Savoie, to La Giettaz, in Savoie, at 1,486 m altitude. Lowest crossing point of the Aravis range, clear views of the Mont Blanc massif, Sainte-Anne chapel, alpine pastures, hiking trailheads.

About Col des Aravis

The Col des Aravis is a road pass at 1,486 m that separates the commune of La Clusaz, in Haute-Savoie, from that of La Giettaz, in Savoie. It is the lowest crossing point through the Aravis range, a massif stretching 25 km north to south from Bonneville to Ugine, culminating at Pointe Percée (2,752 m).

The pass is crossed by the D909, opened in 1902, which today forms a stage on the Route des Grandes Alpes® linking Thonon-les-Bains to Nice. On site: the chapel of Sainte-Anne, restaurants, souvenir shops, alpine farms with direct Reblochon sales, car parks and trailheads. In clear weather, an unobstructed view of the Mont Blanc massif.

How to get there

By car

From La Clusaz, take the D909 towards Flumet / La Giettaz, 7 km to the pass. From Thônes via the north-west: 18.9 km on the D909 (Fier valley). From Flumet via the south-east: 11.5 km on the D909 (Val d'Arly). The road is occasionally closed in winter during heavy snowfall.

By bike

Classic alpine cycling climb. From Flumet: 11.7 km, average gradient ~4.9 %. From Thônes: 18.9 km, average gradient ~4.6 %. A stage pass on the Route des Grandes Alpes® (Thonon-les-Bains → Nice).

When to come

When to visit

Season by season:

  • May – June: green alpine pastures, cows back on the high pastures, quiet pass.
  • July – August: high season, hiking, mountain biking, cyclosportives. Heavy footfall at the pass.
  • September – October: autumn light, herds still on the high pastures until the 'désalpe'.
  • Winter: the pass is reachable by road most of the time, occasionally closed by heavy snowfall. A more wild, quiet atmosphere.

The pass is regularly crossed by the Tour de France and by the Time Megève Mont-Blanc cyclosportive.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Where is the Col des Aravis?

The Col des Aravis sits at 1,486 m altitude between La Clusaz (Haute-Savoie, 74) and La Giettaz (Savoie, 73), on the D909. It marks the boundary between the two departments.

Is access to the Col des Aravis paid?

No. Access to the Col des Aravis is open and free; the pass is crossed by a public-road département route. Free parking on site.

What is the altitude of the Col des Aravis?

The Col des Aravis tops out at 1,486 m, making it the lowest road crossing through the Aravis range.

Is the Col des Aravis open in winter?

Yes, the Col des Aravis road (D909) stays open most of the winter. It may be closed exceptionally during heavy snowfall. Check road conditions before setting out.

Can you see Mont Blanc from the Col des Aravis?

Yes, in clear weather. The Col des Aravis offers an unobstructed view of the Mont Blanc massif, making it one of the busiest road-side viewpoints in the area.

Can you hike from the Col des Aravis?

Yes. Several trails start from the Col des Aravis, including the Croix de Fer loop and the Chalet du Curé on the La Giettaz side, and the climb to the Porte des Aravis. Check with the tourist office for conditions and waymarking.

Is the Col des Aravis a Tour de France stage?

Yes, the Col des Aravis has historically been crossed by the Tour de France, most recently in 2020 according to available sources. It is also on the Time Megève Mont-Blanc cyclosportive route.

Can you buy Reblochon directly at the Col des Aravis?

Yes. Several alpine farms at the Col des Aravis sell directly in season. Reblochon is the historic produce of the Aravis range.

Cascade d'Angon

The Cascade d'Angon drops 60 metres into a limestone canyon above Lake Annecy. It is formed by the confluence of the Nant d'Oy and the Nant de Grenant. The trail, fitted with railings and chains near the falls, is accessible from the hamlet of Vérel, from Angon and from Talloires.

Qu'est-ce que Cascade d'Angon ?

The Cascade d'Angon is a 60-metre waterfall located in the hamlet of Angon, in the commune of Talloires-Montmin, on the foothills of the Bornes-Aravis massif. It is formed by the confluence of the Nant d'Oy — whose source lies on the slopes of La Tournette — and the Nant de Grenant, which descends from the Lanfonnet. Both streams flow into Lake Annecy near Angon beach. The waterfall drops into a limestone canyon. The trail leading to it runs along a cliff face and is secured by metal railings and chains in its final section.

Access is free and open. The trail is signposted with yellow arrows from Angon (Route des Vignes) and from the hamlet of Vérel (Chemin de la Closette). In 2023, the Angon footbridge was fully renovated by the commune's technical services and two external contractors. In October 2024, the Agglomération du Grand Annecy carried out further work on the trail's safety equipment, including the construction of steps at the first waterfall.

Ce qu'on peut y faire

Hiking — loop from Angon

Distance: 4.5 km. Ascent: 384 m. Maximum altitude: 769 m. Estimated time: 2h30. Difficulty: moderate. Start: Route des Vignes, Angon, Talloires-Montmin. Source: Office de Tourisme du Lac d'Annecy.

Short walk from Vérel

Start at Chemin de la Closette, hamlet of Vérel. Round trip of approximately 45 min to 1h. The 'Cascade' bus stop is directly adjacent to the car park.

Canyoning

The Angon canyon has been used for canyoning since 1989 (first documented descent by J. Espejo). Limestone rock. Main waterfall 60 m, second waterfall 25 m. Guided descents only, with certified professional guides.

Rock climbing

A climbing wall is located on the trail between Vérel and the waterfall, suitable for beginners and children.

Comment y aller

En voiture

From Annecy, take the D909 along the eastern shore of the lake to Talloires. Continue 1 km past Talloires and turn left towards Angon (Route des Vignes) for the lower start. For the upper start: after Menthon-Saint-Bernard, turn left towards Col de la Forclaz; at the entrance to the hamlet of Vérel (3 km), turn right onto Chemin de la Closette.

En transports en commun

SIBRA bus line 20, stop Angon — Route des Vignes (lower trailhead). Stop Cascade at Vérel in season. Departs from Annecy train station. Source: Commune de Talloires-Montmin.

À vélo

Cycle path accessible to the Angon trailhead from Annecy. Electric bike recommended for Vérel.

Quand venir ?

Water flow is highest in spring, during snowmelt. In winter, the waterfall can freeze partially or completely. The trail is not recommended in wet weather or in the hours following rainfall, as the rocky surface near the falls becomes very slippery. The Office de Tourisme du Lac d'Annecy indicates the trail is accessible year-round subject to favourable weather conditions.

Questions fréquentes

Is the Cascade d'Angon free to visit?
Yes, access to the Cascade d'Angon is free and open. The car parks at Angon and Vérel are paid from May to end of September.
How do I get to the Cascade d'Angon from Annecy by public transport?
SIBRA bus line 20 connects Annecy train station to Talloires-Montmin with a stop at Angon — Route des Vignes. During the summer season, a stop called Cascade also serves the upper trailhead at Vérel.
How tall is the Cascade d'Angon?
The Cascade d'Angon is 60 metres high. It is formed by the confluence of the Nant d'Oy and the Nant de Grenant.
Can you swim at the Cascade d'Angon?
Swimming is prohibited at the Cascade d'Angon. It is possible to swim in the river approximately 10 to 15 minutes upstream, provided the flow is low enough.
Is the Cascade d'Angon suitable for children?
The walk from Vérel (upper trailhead) is accessible to families with children in reasonable physical condition. The trail near the Cascade d'Angon is rocky and slippery; children must be held by the hand in the final section.
How long does the hike to the Cascade d'Angon take from Angon?
From the Angon car park (Route des Vignes), allow 1h30 to 2h return to reach the Cascade d'Angon. The full loop via Vérel and the Pont des Fées is estimated at 2h30 for 4.5 km and 384 m of ascent.
Is canyoning possible at the Cascade d'Angon?
Yes, the Angon canyon has been used for canyoning since 1989. The descent includes the 60-metre main waterfall and a second 25-metre cascade. It is carried out with certified professional guides only.
What is the best time to visit the Cascade d'Angon?
The Cascade d'Angon has the strongest flow in spring during snowmelt, and autumn is also a good time. The trail should be avoided after rain due to slippery conditions. In winter, the Cascade d'Angon may be partially or fully frozen.

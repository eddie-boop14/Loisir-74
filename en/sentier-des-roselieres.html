What is Reedbed Trail

The Reedbed Trail is a boardwalk laid out by the Saint-Jorioz commune along the shore of Lake Annecy. About 1 km long, it crosses one of the largest reedbeds on the western shore, a buffer between the lake and the wet meadows.

The wooden boardwalk on stilts lets visitors walk through the reeds without damaging the fragile substrate. Interpretation panels along the way explain how reedbeds form, their role in water quality and biodiversity (pike spawning grounds, little bittern nesting, reed warbler song). You can regularly spot mute swans, coots, great crested grebes, herons, and in spring the yellow flag iris in bloom.

The walk takes 30 minutes to 1 hour, out-and-back or as a loop depending on the variant. It is free and open year-round, an ideal stop on a family day out on the western shore, combined with the Saint-Jorioz municipal beach, the Lake Annecy greenway or a walk up to the Roc de Chère.

How to get there

By car

From Annecy: D1508 along the west shore, 15 min to Saint-Jorioz. From Geneva: A41 exit Annecy Sud, then D1508, about 50 min.

Public transport

SIBRA / Y'BUS lines from Annecy to Saint-Jorioz; the trail is 10 min walk from the central stop.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is the trail paid?

No, free access year-round.

Is it stroller-accessible?

Largely yes, on wide slats. A few sections are narrower — all-terrain stroller advised.

Are dogs allowed?

On a leash, yes; avoid during nesting (April-June) to protect the birds.

How long should I plan?

30 min for a quick stroll, 1 h if you observe wildlife and read the panels.

Can I swim?

Not directly from the trail; Saint-Jorioz municipal beach is right next door.

Is it open in winter?

Yes, except in heavy frost which may make the boardwalk slippery — take care.

How to combine with a lake day?

Ideal at the start or end of the day, before a picnic at the beach, a pedalo ride or a greenway cycle.

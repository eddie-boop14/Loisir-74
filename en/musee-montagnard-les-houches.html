Musée Montagnard des Houches

Folk arts and traditions museum in a 1750 house in the heart of Les Houches: collection bequeathed by architect Albert Laprade, traditional rooms reconstructed.

What is the Musée Montagnard des Houches?

The Musée Montagnard des Houches is a folk arts and traditions museum housed in a heritage building from 1750 in the heart of the Les Houches village, opposite the church. Managed by the Chamonix-Mont-Blanc Valley Community of Communes (CCVCMB), in the same network as the future Musée du Mont-Blanc and the Espace Tairraz, it benefits from the CCVCMB Museum Pass at €25/year giving unlimited access to the 6 sites in the network.

The collection was bequeathed by architect-urbanist Albert Laprade, member of the Institut. The tour presents reconstructed traditional rooms of the alpine house from the 18th to 20th century: pèle (living room), outa (kitchen), sarto (cellar), attic, with agropastoral objects from 1750-1950: tools, furniture, traditional costumes, everyday utensils.

The temporary exhibition 'Cable cars and company' traces the history of cable cars in Les Houches since the 1936 Bellevue installation, in partnership with the 'Les Houches dans l'temps' association. ALERT as of 23 May 2026: MUSEUM CLOSED IN MAY 2026 (winter-Easter holidays window ended on 8 March 2026, exhibition closed on 16 May). Reopens in summer 2026. Prices: adult €5, reduced rate €4, free for under-18s.

Frequently asked questions

How do I book at the Musée Montagnard des Houches?
The Musée Montagnard des Houches operates with free access during opening hours during school holidays and July-August. Outside these periods: visits by booking Mon-Fri (≥2 people) by telephone +33 (0)7 60 04 14 26 or email musee-montagnard@ccvcmb.fr.
What is the price of the Musée Montagnard des Houches?
Musée Montagnard des Houches: full adult price €5, reduced rate €4 (ViaCham, guest card, student, 65+, reduced mobility, jobseeker), group (10+) €4/€3. Free under 18. CCVCMB Museum Pass €25/year. Verified on 23 May 2026.
What are the opening hours of the Musée Montagnard des Houches?
Musée Montagnard des Houches: school holidays Wed-Thu-Fri 2pm-6pm. July-August every day except Tuesday 2.30pm-6pm. Outside holidays by appointment Mon-Fri. Verified on the official website on 23 May 2026 — subject to seasonal changes. Closed in May 2026.
Is the Musée Montagnard des Houches currently open?
No. The Musée Montagnard des Houches is closed in May 2026 between the end of the winter-Easter holidays (8 March 2026) and the summer 2026 resumption. The 'Cable cars and company' exhibition closed on 16 May 2026 and resumes for the 2026-2027 cycle.
Who was Albert Laprade at the Musée Montagnard des Houches?
Albert Laprade (1883-1978) was a French architect-urbanist, member of the Institut, who bequeathed his folk arts and traditions collection to the Musée Montagnard des Houches. Agropastoral objects 1750-1950, furniture, traditional costumes.
Is there a Museum Pass at the Musée Montagnard des Houches?
Yes. The CCVCMB Museum Pass at €25/year gives unlimited access to the Musée Montagnard des Houches and the 5 other sites in the network: future Musée du Mont-Blanc, Espace Tairraz, Maison de la Mémoire J. Couttet, Maison du Lieutenant in Servoz, Maison de Barberine in Vallorcine.
Is the Musée Montagnard des Houches accessible to people with reduced mobility?
Partial reduced-mobility accessibility at the Musée Montagnard des Houches: entrance via the rear of the building, lift to the 1st floor. 1750 heritage building with structural constraints. Reduced rate for reduced mobility / disabled visitors €4 on proof.
How do I get to the Musée Montagnard des Houches from Chamonix?
The Musée Montagnard des Houches is 8 km from the centre of Chamonix. By train: Mont-Blanc Express to Les Houches station (700 m from the museum). By bus: Chamonix Bus stop 'Les Houches Mairie'. By car: A40 exit Les Houches, free car parks Mairie and Freney.

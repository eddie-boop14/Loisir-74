What is CGN Évian Stop

The CGN Évian stop serves the year-round N1 Lausanne–Évian line on Lake Geneva, operated by the Compagnie Générale de Navigation sur le Léman, the historic operator founded in 1873. The crossing takes about 35 minutes. Boarding is at Quai Albert Besson in the port of Évian-les-Bains.

The CGN runs the most prestigious Belle Époque fleet in the world: five historic late-19th to early-20th-century steamers (Italie, Vevey, La Suisse, Simplon, Savoie), classified as Swiss historic monuments and still in service, complemented by modern wheelchair-accessible boats.

2026 fares: adult from €16.80 (single); free under 5, reduced fare under 16. Year-round service; high-season frequencies, reduced in winter — check cgn.ch. Modern boats are wheelchair-accessible.

About ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais is a greenway / EV17 cycle route at Thonon-les-Bains (74200) in Haute-Savoie. Bas-Chablais leg of the ViaRhôna EV17 — 26 km along Lake Léman from Thonon to Chens-sur-Léman via Sciez, Excenevex (sandy beach) and Yvoire (medieval village).

The Bas-Chablais leg of the ViaRhôna EV17 is 26 km along Lake Léman between Thonon-les-Bains and Chens-sur-Léman via Sciez, Excenevex and Yvoire. Mix of short greenway sections (2 km leaving Thonon, 2 km at Excenevex) + quiet shared roads with EV17 waymarking.

The Bas-Chablais leg of the ViaRhôna EV17 directly serves Yvoire, a 14th-century medieval village listed among the Plus Beaux Villages de France. Ramparts, castle, Jardin des 5 Sens (already published as a Loisirs 74 venue).

FREE OPEN ACCESS year-round. Difficulty easy. Length 26 km · Bas-Chablais leg, duration 1 h 30 – 2 h by bike. Recommended season: year-round. Reduced-mobility access: mostly (short greenway sections). Loisirs 74 cross-links: ViaRhôna Haute-Savoie parent page (venue #2), Yvoire Jardin des 5 Sens (already published museum), Excenevex beach (forthcoming on Loisirs 74).

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is access to the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais paid?

No. Access to the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais is free and open year-round for all. No ticketing, no toll. Open at all times subject to season and weather.

How long is the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais is 26 km · Bas-Chablais leg. Average duration 1 h 30 – 2 h by bike depending on fitness. Easy. Greenway-type route / EV17 cycle route.

What are the opening hours of the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais is free open access 24/7 year-round. Recommended year-round. Verified on the official website on 24 May 2026 — subject to seasonal changes.

How hard is the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais is easy. Length 26 km · Bas-Chablais leg. Duration 1 h 30 – 2 h by bike. Altitude 374 m (Lake Léman shore). For hikers depending on fitness profile.

Is the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais accessible for visitors with reduced mobility?

Reduced-mobility access: mostly (short greenway sections) for the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais. Check conditions on site. For an accessible alternative see the Lake Annecy greenway (venue #1) or the Sentier des Roselières at Saint-Jorioz (venue #10) if not suitable.

When can you ride the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

Recommended season for the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais: year-round. Conditions outside the season vary with altitude and snow cover. Check conditions with the local tourist office before travelling.

How do I get to the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais starts from Thonon-les-Bains (74200). Car recommended for access to the trailhead. Free parking at trailheads. Public transport varies by line and season.

What other sites can you combine with the ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais?

The ViaRhôna EV17 Bas-Chablais combines with the ViaRhôna Haute-Savoie parent page (venue #2) and other thematic venues in the region. Recommended cross-links for a full day.

Is the Saint-Gingolph – Thonon section suitable for families?

Not really: it uses many départemental roads. For families, it's better to start from Thonon or Geneva.

What is Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail

The Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail is an educational route around the Lac des Plagnes, a charming mountain lake at around 1,170 m altitude, in the commune of Abondance in the Vallée d'Abondance. The trail belongs to the Chablais UNESCO Global Geopark: its interpretive panels explain 20,000 years of landscape evolution, from the great Quaternary glaciers to today's slowly rising peat bogs.

The route begins with a loop around the lake on a flat, shaded path, then continues upstream along the lake's tributary, climbing through the forest beside a waterfall to the Cubourré plateau, drained by a small stream and dominated by the Pointe d'Entre Deux Pertuis and the Chavache. A wide track then leads to a mysterious erratic boulder whose origin is explained on site. The return is by the same direct path to the car park. Allow 2 to 3 hours depending on the option (lake loop alone, or lake loop plus Cubourré climb). Free access, swimming forbidden, a fine outing for families between geological heritage and nature observation.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Where is the Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail?

The Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail starts at the lake car park, above the village of Abondance, in the Vallée d'Abondance, at about 1,170 m altitude.

Is the Lac des Plagnes Trail difficult?

The loop around the Lac des Plagnes is easy and flat (30-45 min). The option that climbs to the Cubourré plateau and the erratic boulder is more demanding (2 to 3 hrs round trip) with elevation through the forest alongside the waterfall.

Can you swim on the Lac des Plagnes Trail?

No, swimming is forbidden at the Lac des Plagnes: it is a protected natural area within the Chablais UNESCO Global Geopark. You can however picnic on its shores.

What does the Lac des Plagnes Trail tell?

The Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail tells 20,000 years of geological history of the Vallée d'Abondance: great glaciers, slow retreat, peat bog formation, and the story of an intriguing erratic boulder left on site.

Is the Lac des Plagnes Trail suitable for children?

Yes. The loop around the Lac des Plagnes is flat and suitable for children old enough to walk. The Cubourré option is more demanding but remains accessible to families with children used to walking.

When can you walk the Lac des Plagnes Trail?

The Lac des Plagnes Interpretive Trail is practicable from May to October depending on snow cover, with free access year-round once the road is cleared. In winter, the area is used for snowshoeing.

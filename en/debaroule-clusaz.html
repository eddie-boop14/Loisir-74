What is Débaroule — Playful Trail

The Débaroule is a playful high-altitude trail laid out on the La Clusaz ski domain, accessible in summer from the top of the Patinoire cable car. Its twist: walkers descend on foot to the Bossonnet, accompanying their own wooden ball along the way through 7 playful wooden modules that make the ball roll, drop and run — using mechanical principles reminiscent of giant marble runs (wooden flippers, ramps, turns, accelerators, launches).

The experience is designed for families, suitable for children from 3–4 with an adult. The trail is easy and downhill, with no technical difficulty. The route is part of La Clusaz's summer activity package and the "Pass Découverte" combining foot-passenger lifts and several summer activities.

At the end of the Débaroule, children can carry on to the nearby Luge des Bois at Bossonnet. Summer season (July–August + weekends).

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the Débaroule at La Clusaz?

The Débaroule is a playful high-altitude trail in La Clusaz: from the top of the Patinoire cable car, walkers descend on foot to the Bossonnet, accompanying their wooden ball through 7 playful modules.

How long does the Débaroule at La Clusaz take?

The Débaroule at La Clusaz takes 30 to 45 minutes to descend, depending on your pace and the time spent at each module. Longer if continued with the Luge des Bois at the Bossonnet.

How much does the Débaroule at La Clusaz cost?

The Débaroule at La Clusaz is included in the summer Pass Découverte or accessible with a pedestrian pass for the Patinoire cable car. 2026 prices to be confirmed at forfait.laclusaz.com. Verified 10 June 2026.

What are the opening times of the Débaroule at La Clusaz?

The Débaroule at La Clusaz runs during the summer season (mid-June/early July to end of August), depending on the summer lift calendar. Check forfait.laclusaz.com. Verified 10 June 2026.

Is the Débaroule at La Clusaz suitable for children?

Yes, the Débaroule at La Clusaz is designed for families with children from age 3-4 (accompanied). Easy descending trail, child-friendly playful modules and wooden balls provided.

How do I access the Débaroule at La Clusaz?

Access the Débaroule at La Clusaz via the Patinoire cable car (parking in the village), with a pedestrian pass or the Pass Découverte. The descent leads down to the Bossonnet.

Can the Débaroule be combined with other activities?

Yes, on arrival of the Débaroule at the Bossonnet you can continue with the Luge des Bois — another flagship summer attraction in La Clusaz, also included in the Pass Découverte.

Do I need to book for the Débaroule at La Clusaz?

No direct booking for the Débaroule. Tickets at the Patinoire cable car in La Clusaz or online at forfait.laclusaz.com (pedestrian pass or Pass Découverte).

What is Les Houches

Les Houches is a family-friendly village resort (Famille Plus label) at the entrance to the Vallée de Chamonix-Mont-Blanc, offering a panoramic view of the Mont-Blanc range and the Aiguilles de Chamonix.

Its ski area, linked with Saint-Gervais, spans between 1 000 and 1 900 m in altitude over around 55 km of pistes laid out mostly through forest — an area-wide figure, shared with the neighbouring sector. Access from the village is via the Prarion gondola and the Bellevue cable car.

The resort is world-famous for la Verte des Houches, the Kandahar piste, venue for the men's Alpine Skiing World Cup downhill. A beginners' area is set up at Tourchet, in the heart of the village, and a Nordic skiing trail is offered on the Prarion plateau. SKICAMP offers sledging, snow tubing and a zip line, and marked snowshoeing trails are available. In winter, the Tramway du Mont-Blanc also serves the Bellevue plateau.

Les Houches is also one of the sectors of the greater Vallée de Chamonix-Mont-Blanc and the official starting point of the Tour du Mont-Blanc, with a car park dedicated to hikers (parking des Chavants). Nearby, the Parc de Merlet, a former mountain pasture at 1 500 m, lets visitors observe ibex, chamois and mouflon roaming semi-freely — hiking, mountain biking, climbing and the Base de Loisirs des Chavants round out the off-piste offering.

Access

How to get there

By car

Les Houches is reached via the A40 motorway, exit n°27 « Les Houches Centre », then the RN205 (Route Blanche / Route Mont-Blanc) which runs through the commune, at the entrance to the Chamonix valley.

The commune has its own SNCF station on the Mont-Blanc Express line (Saint-Gervais–Le Fayet / Chamonix / Vallorcine). Locally, the Skibus of the Chamonix Mobilité network, free for holders of a ski pass and a guest card, serves Les Houches from Chamonix and the valley's other villages.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is Les Houches part of the Chamonix ski area?

Les Houches is one of the ski sectors of the Vallée de Chamonix-Mont-Blanc, alongside Brévent-Flégère, Les Grands Montets and Le Tour-Balme. Its own ski area is linked with that of Saint-Gervais, between 1 000 and 1 900 m.

What are la Verte des Houches and the Kandahar?

La Verte des Houches is the Kandahar piste, venue for the men's Alpine Skiing World Cup downhill that made Les Houches world-famous.

How can you reach Les Houches without a car?

Les Houches has its own SNCF station on the Mont-Blanc Express line. Locally, the Skibus of the Chamonix Mobilité network, free for holders of a ski pass and a guest card, serves the village from Chamonix and the valley's other villages.

What can you do in Les Houches besides skiing?

The Parc de Merlet (wildlife park at 1 500 m facing Mont-Blanc), the Musée Montagnard des Houches and the official starting point of the Tour du Mont-Blanc (parking des Chavants) round out the off-piste offering, with hiking, mountain biking and climbing.

What is Mont-Blanc Padel — Passy

Mont-Blanc Padel is a padel centre in Passy, in the Arve valley, very close to the Mont-Blanc massif. The club offers several indoor courts — allowing year-round, all-weather play — with glass walls, changing rooms and a friendly reception. Padel, a racquet sport mixing tennis and squash, is typically played in doubles on a 10 × 20 m court enclosed by glass and mesh.

Who is it for? The centre suits beginners (group intro lessons, racquet rental) as well as experienced players (free booking, tournaments, intermediate/advanced levels). Private lessons with a coach, children's/teen camps during school holidays, corporate events and birthdays are also available. Padel is one of the fastest-growing sports, accessible and fun, ideal for friend groups.

Booking and good to know. Online booking (via padel apps like TeniUp or directly with the club) strongly recommended, especially evenings and weekends. Prices around €20-30/hour for court rental (shared between 4 players), with hour passes and subscriptions. Racquet rental available (~€5). The centre is near Passy, accessible by car (free parking) from Sallanches/Le Fayet or Saint-Gervais.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is padel?

Padel is a fast-growing racquet sport, a mix of tennis and squash. It's played in doubles on a 10 × 20 m court enclosed by glass and mesh that players can use to bounce the ball. Accessible and very fun.

Do I need to be a tennis player?

No, padel is very beginner-friendly. The basics come in one or two sessions, and the smaller court and solid (stringless) racquet make it quick to pick up.

How much does a game cost?

Renting a court for 1h costs around €20-30 (shared between 4 players, so €5-7/person). Lessons from ~€15-20/person. Racquet rental ~€5. Exact 2026 prices to confirm with the club.

How do I book?

Booking is online via padel apps (TeniUp, etc.) or directly with the club. Strongly recommended evenings and weekends, when slots fill up fast.

Is there gear on site?

Yes, the club offers racquet and ball rental (~€5). For beginners, this is ideal to try without investing in your own equipment.

Can I take lessons?

Yes, the club offers group intro lessons (~€15-20/person) and private lessons with a coach to progress faster. Children/teen camps during school holidays.

What should I wear?

Comfortable sportswear, sports shoes (ideally padel or indoor tennis shoes with non-marking soles). Water bottle recommended.

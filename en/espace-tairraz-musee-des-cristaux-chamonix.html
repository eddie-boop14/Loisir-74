Espace Tairraz

Musée des Cristaux renovated in 2021 and Espace Alpinisme in the heart of Chamonix: nearly 1,900 mineralogical pieces from Mont-Blanc and the Northern Alps.

What is Espace Tairraz ?

Espace Tairraz is a twin museum located in the heart of Chamonix, on the forecourt of the Saint-Michel car park: it combines the Musée des Cristaux (renovated in 2021) and the Espace Alpinisme. Managed by the Communauté de Communes de la Vallée de Chamonix-Mont-Blanc (CCVCMB) within the same network as the future Musée du Mont-Blanc and the Maison de la Mémoire Janny Couttet, it benefits from the CCVCMB Pass Musées at €25/year giving unlimited access to the 6 sites of the valley.

The Musée des Cristaux presents nearly 1,900 mineralogical pieces from the collections of the Musée Alpin and Club de Minéralogie of Chamonix, Mont-Blanc and the Northern Alps. The collection is internationally renowned, with an exceptional set of pink-red fluorites, smoky quartz, axinites, siderites, epidotes, largely from the Mont-Blanc massif. The temporary exhibition 'Trésor minéral: la collection Raymond Thibault' runs until December 2027.

Clear pricing: adult €8, reduced rate €5.50 (guest card, residents, 65+, students, large families, visitors with reduced mobility, jobseekers), free under 18. Guided tours Thursdays at 2.15pm and 4.15pm during French school holidays, by a 'cristallier' from the Club de Minéralogie de Chamonix. Espace Tairraz is easily accessible to visitors with reduced mobility from the Saint-Michel car park via a direct lift.

Frequently asked questions

How do I book at Espace Tairraz?
Espace Tairraz operates on open access during opening hours, with no booking for individual visits. Guided tours and groups (10+) by reservation via email musee-cristaux@ccvcmb.fr or by phone +33 (0)4 50 54 78 39.
What is the price of Espace Tairraz?
Espace Tairraz: full adult rate €8, reduced rate €5.50 (guest card, residents, 65+, students, large families, visitors with reduced mobility, jobseekers). Group (10+) €5.50/€3.60. Free under 18. CCVCMB Pass Musées €25/year (6 sites). Verified on 23 May 2026.
What are the opening hours of Espace Tairraz?
Espace Tairraz: 4 May – 30 June 2026 Tuesday-Sunday 2pm-6pm, closed Monday. July-August Tuesday-Sunday 10am-1pm and 2pm-6pm. Closed 1 Jan, 1 May, 25 Dec. Verified on the official site on 23 May 2026 — subject to seasonal changes.
What can be seen at Espace Tairraz?
Espace Tairraz presents the Musée des Cristaux (1,900 mineralogical pieces from the Mont-Blanc massif and the Northern Alps, renovated in 2021) and the Espace Alpinisme. Collections of the Club de Minéralogie de Chamonix: fluorites, smoky quartz, axinites, siderites.
Is there a Pass Musées at Espace Tairraz?
Yes. The CCVCMB Pass Musées at €25/year gives unlimited access to Espace Tairraz and the 5 other sites in the network: future Musée du Mont-Blanc (July 2026), Maison de la Mémoire J. Couttet, Musée Montagnard in Les Houches, Maison du Lieutenant in Servoz, Maison de Barberine in Vallorcine.
Is Espace Tairraz accessible for visitors with reduced mobility?
Yes, excellent accessibility. Espace Tairraz is fully wheelchair-accessible via a direct lift from the Saint-Michel car park. Path with sloped sections from the Tourist Office. Reduced rate €5.50 for visitors with reduced mobility.
Does Espace Tairraz offer guided tours?
Yes. Espace Tairraz offers guided tours by a 'cristallier' from the Club de Minéralogie de Chamonix on Thursdays at 2.15pm and 4.15pm during French school holidays. Field anecdotes, identification demonstrations, sharing of passion.
How do I get to Espace Tairraz from the station?
Espace Tairraz is 400 m from Chamonix-Mont-Blanc SNCF station (Mont-Blanc Express). 6 min on foot. By bus: Chamonix Bus stop 'Chamonix Centre'. Mulet shuttle stop 'Casino'. Saint-Michel car park directly under the museum with a lift.

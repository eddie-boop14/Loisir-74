About Espace Tairraz

The Espace Tairraz is a twin museum in the heart of Chamonix, on the forecourt of the Saint-Michel car park: it combines the Musée des Cristaux (refurbished in 2021) and the Espace Alpinisme. Run by the Communauté de Communes de la Vallée de Chamonix-Mont-Blanc (CCVCMB) in the same network as the future Musée du Mont-Blanc and the Janny Couttet Maison de la Mémoire, it is covered by the CCVCMB Pass Musées at €25 a year, giving unlimited access to the valley's 6 sites.

The Musée des Cristaux shows nearly 1,900 mineralogical pieces from the collections of the Musée alpin and the Chamonix, Mont-Blanc and Northern Alps Mineralogy Club. The collection has an international reputation, with an exceptional set of pink-red fluorites, smoky quartz, axinites, siderites, epidotes, largely from the Mont Blanc massif. The temporary exhibition 'Mineral Treasure: the Raymond Thibault collection' runs until December 2027.

Clear pricing: adult €8, reduced €5.50 (guest card, resident, 65+, students, large families, reduced-mobility/disabled, jobseekers), free for under 18s. Guided tours on Thursdays at 2.15 pm and 4.15 pm during French school holidays, led by a crystal hunter from the Chamonix Mineralogy Club. The Espace Tairraz is easily accessible for reduced-mobility visitors from the Saint-Michel car park via a direct lift.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How do I book at the Espace Tairraz?

The Espace Tairraz is free entry during opening hours, with no booking required for individual visits. Guided tours and groups (10+) by booking by email musee-cristaux@ccvcmb.fr or phone +33 (0)4 50 54 78 39.

How much does the Espace Tairraz cost?

Espace Tairraz: full adult €8, reduced €5.50 (guest card, resident, 65+, students, large families, reduced-mobility/disabled, jobseekers). Group (10+) €5.50/€3.60. Free under 18. CCVCMB Pass Musées €25 per year (6 sites). Verified 23 May 2026.

What are the opening hours of the Espace Tairraz?

Espace Tairraz: 4 May – 30 June 2026 Tuesday-Sunday 2 pm – 6 pm, closed Monday. July-August Tuesday-Sunday 10 am – 1 pm and 2 pm – 6 pm. Closed 1 January, 1 May, 25 December. Verified on the official website on 23 May 2026 — subject to seasonal changes.

What can you see at the Espace Tairraz?

The Espace Tairraz presents the Musée des Cristaux (1,900 mineralogical pieces from the Mont Blanc massif and the Northern Alps, refurbished in 2021) and the Espace Alpinisme. Chamonix Mineralogy Club collections: fluorites, smoky quartz, axinites, siderites.

Is there a Pass Musées at the Espace Tairraz?

Yes. The CCVCMB Pass Musées at €25 a year gives unlimited access to the Espace Tairraz and the 5 other network sites: future Musée du Mont-Blanc (July 2026), J. Couttet Maison de la Mémoire, Musée Montagnard des Houches, Maison du Lieutenant in Servoz, Maison de Barberine in Vallorcine.

Is the Espace Tairraz accessible for visitors with reduced mobility?

Yes — excellent accessibility. The Espace Tairraz is fully wheelchair-accessible via a direct lift from the Saint-Michel car park. Sloped sections from the tourist office. Reduced rate €5.50 for reduced-mobility/disabled visitors.

Does the Espace Tairraz offer guided tours?

Yes. The Espace Tairraz offers guided tours led by a crystal hunter from the Chamonix Mineralogy Club on Thursdays at 2.15 pm and 4.15 pm during French school holidays. Field anecdotes, identification demonstrations, sharing of passion.

How do I get to the Espace Tairraz from the station?

The Espace Tairraz is 400 m from the Chamonix-Mont-Blanc SNCF station (Mont-Blanc Express). 6 min on foot. By bus: Chamonix Bus, stop 'Chamonix Centre'. Mulet shuttle stop 'Casino'. Parking Saint-Michel directly beneath the museum with a lift.

Free for children?

Yes, free for under 18s.

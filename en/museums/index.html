Museums

Art, history, science and craft — Haute-Savoie indoors, to discover in any weather.

When the weather turns, or simply out of curiosity, the museums of Haute-Savoie tell the story of the mountains, Savoyard history, art and science — from the medieval fortress to the interactive centre.

Read more

In Annecy, the Musée-Château and the Palais de l'Île overlook the old town; La Turbine sciences takes a playful, interactive approach, ideal for families.

Elsewhere, the animation cinema museum, the Espace Tairraz in Chamonix (mountaineering and crystals) or the open-air heritage museums bring local know-how and history back to life.

Most open all year, with reduced hours out of season. Each listing shows the access, the commune and an official link.

A safe bet on rainy days: the majority of museums are fully indoors.

Frequently asked questions

Which museums to visit in Annecy?
The Musée-Château d'Annecy and the Palais de l'Île, in the heart of the old town, are the most emblematic; La Turbine sciences rounds things out for families.
Are there museums for children?
Yes: La Turbine sciences (interactive), the animation cinema museum and several heritage museums offer family-friendly visits.
Are the museums open all year?
Most open all year, with reduced hours out of season. Check the official site of each museum.
What to do in Haute-Savoie when it rains?
Museums are a safe bet in the rain, as are the water parks, cinemas and indoor activities.
Which museum covers the mountains and mountaineering?
The Espace Tairraz in Chamonix traces the history of mountaineering and displays a collection of crystals from the Mont-Blanc massif.

What is Château de Buffavent

The Château de Buffavent is a 15th-century fortified manor set on a natural terrace about 800 metres north-west of the village of Lully (74890), in the Chablais. Its evocative name reportedly comes from "bouffâ" — to blow in Savoyard dialect — pointing to a wind-exposed site. The trapezoidal main building is about 18.6 m long by 15.8 m wide, with four round corner towers crowned by conical "pepper-pot" roofs, characteristic of 15th-century Savoyard military architecture.

The castle was built by the lords of Langin as a fortified hunting residence, during the expansion phase that peaked in the 15th–16th centuries. In 1590, Genevan soldiers attacked the castle during the wars between Savoy and Genevan Protestantism: the arrow slits of the left tower still bear traces of cannon fire.

The château is private property, not open to the public, but is easily visible from the surrounding paths around Lully. Listed as a Monument Historique in 1944.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Can you visit the inside of the Château de Buffavent?

No, the Château de Buffavent is a private property closed to interior visits. The silhouette of the fortified manor and its four pepper-pot towers are clearly visible from the road and the local paths of Lully.

Where is the Château de Buffavent?

The Château de Buffavent stands about 800 m north-west of the village of Lully (74890), in the Bas-Chablais, 15 km from Thonon-les-Bains. GPS: 46.288° N · 6.406° E.

What is the history of the Château de Buffavent?

The Château de Buffavent is a 15th-century fortified manor built by the lords of Langin as a fortified hunting residence. Attacked and taken by the Genevans in 1590 (cannonball marks visible), it was listed as a Historic Monument in 1944.

How much does it cost to visit the Château de Buffavent?

Looking at the Château de Buffavent from outside is free from the public road. There is no interior visit, as the castle is privately owned. Checked on 10 June 2026.

What are the opening hours of the Château de Buffavent?

The exterior view of the Château de Buffavent is freely accessible year-round, 24/7, from the road and the local paths. No scheduled interior visit. Checked on 10 June 2026.

How long should I allow to see the Château de Buffavent?

Allow 15 to 30 minutes to admire the Château de Buffavent from outside, photograph the four pepper-pot towers and read about its history. Longer if you combine it with a country walk.

Where can I park to see the Château de Buffavent?

Free parking in the village of Lully. The Château de Buffavent is then reachable on foot via the road or the local paths, about 800 m from the village.

What else is there to see around the Château de Buffavent?

Around the Château de Buffavent, you can discover the Château d'Avully at Brenthonne (5 km away), the villages of the Bas-Chablais, and the paths leading to Thonon-les-Bains and Lake Geneva.

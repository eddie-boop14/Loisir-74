Where to swim in Lake Geneva (French side)?

The public beaches of the Chablais ranked by real cost — entry and parking.

On the French side, Lake Geneva lines up 11 public beaches. 8 are free, 1 has paid parking in season (Messery) and 2 have paid entry in summer: the Centre nautique d'Évian (5,50 €) and the municipal beach of Thonon (5,70 €). Excenevex boasts the lake's only large sandy beach.

Frequently asked questions

Which beaches on Lake Geneva are free?

Most of them: eight public beaches offer free, open access, from Tougues (Chens) to Saint-Gingolph, including Excenevex, Sciez, Amphion and la Pinède in Thonon.

Which beaches on Lake Geneva charge an entry fee?

Two, in summer only: the Centre nautique d'Évian (5,50 €, 2025 price) and the municipal beach of Thonon-les-Bains (5,70 €). Messery remains free but its parking is paid in season.

Where to find a sandy beach on Lake Geneva?

In Excenevex: it's the only large natural fine-sand beach on Lake Geneva, very popular in summer.

Which beaches on Lake Geneva have lifeguards?

Lifeguard supervision is seasonal (July-August) at municipal beaches such as Amphion, Saint-Gingolph, Évian and Thonon. Several beaches (Messery, Margencel) offer open swimming without supervision — see each listing.

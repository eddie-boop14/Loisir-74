What is Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais

Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais is a forest adventure park located at Beunaz Beach, at 900 metres altitude. The park offers 7 treetop courses of increasing difficulty, with continuous safety lines for maximum security. The activity is accessible from age 2 (Little Kid course under adult supervision), with courses adapted by height: Little Kid (age 2 to 105 cm), Kid (105 cm to 135 cm), and Indiana Park (from 135 cm, with blue, red and black courses).

The park is operated by Indiana'Ventures, founded in 2001 by Pascal Berger, which manages three adventure sites in Haute-Savoie (Morzine, Samoëns, Saint-Paul-en-Chablais). The Saint-Paul site is part of the Beunaz Beach leisure complex, which offers supervised swimming, water activities (paddle, canoe, pedalo), inflatable structures, Télébeune (zip line crossing the lake), lake-fed swimming pool, diving boards, restaurant and snack bar. The treetop course is an integral part of this offering, allowing visitors to combine multiple activities in a single day.

What sets Indiana'Ventures apart from many Chablais treetop parks is its unique setting beside a natural temperate mountain lake at 900 m altitude. Unlike purely forest parks, families here can alternate morning climbing with afternoon swimming without changing location. The continuous safety line system, on-site professional height safety staff, and graduated offering (from Little Kid to black course) welcome the whole family, from 2-year-old toddlers to adults seeking thrills. The address '1000 route de la plage' reflects the site's multi-activity vocation: lake, beach, forest and leisure coexist harmoniously.

Access

How to get there

By car

From Évian-les-Bains (~15 mins), follow signs for 'Pays de Gavot' then 'Bernex' and 'Beunaz Beach'. From Thonon-les-Bains (~20 mins), take D32 towards Saint-Paul-en-Chablais, then follow signs for 'Beunaz Beach'. Free parking on-site (1000 route de la plage).

When to visit

When to visit

The main season is July and August (daily 10:00–18:00). In June, the park opens weekends and public holidays only. Prefer mornings in July–August to avoid crowds. Beunaz Beach is quieter in June and early July. In very hot weather, combine the treetop course in the morning and swimming in the afternoon. Check the weather before visiting: strong winds or storms may cause the activity to close for safety reasons.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How do I book Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

Book online at indianaventures.com (Saint-Paul-en-Chablais site section) or directly at Beunaz Beach ticket office. Tickets are sold individually for each course. Booking is recommended in peak season (July–August).

What are the prices for Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

Indiana Park (from 135 cm) costs €23. Indiana Kid (105 cm to 135 cm): €17. Little Kid course (age 2 to 105 cm): €11. Léman France Pass: -10%. Verified 6 June 2026.

What are the hours for Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais is open daily from 10:00 to 18:00 in July and August. In June, open weekends and public holidays only. Verified on the official site on 6 June 2026 — subject to seasonal changes.

From what age can children do the treetop course at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

The treetop course at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais is accessible from age 2 with the Little Kid course (up to 105 cm), under adult supervision. From 105 cm, children access the Kid courses independently. From 135 cm, access to Indiana Park courses (blue, red, black).

Are the courses at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais safe for children?

Yes. All courses at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais are equipped with continuous safety lines: the user clips in at the start and can only unclip at the finish. Little Kid courses are supervised by an accompanying adult. Professional height safety staff are on-site.

Can I combine the treetop course with swimming at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

Yes. Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais is located at Beunaz Beach, which offers supervised swimming, paddle, canoe, pedalo, inflatable lake structures, Télébeune (zip line), swimming pool, diving boards, restaurant and snack bar. Perfect for a full day.

When is the best time to visit with young children?

June and early July mornings are ideal for young children: fewer crowds, cooler temperatures for climbing, then swimming in the afternoon. Little Kid and Kid courses are specifically designed for ages 2-12 with supervised safety.

Is there a restaurant on-site at Indiana'Ventures Saint-Paul-en-Chablais?

Yes, restaurant À la Belle Étoile and snack bar 'la case à Caza' are located at Beunaz Beach. You can have lunch, a snack or refreshments between climbing and swimming. Picnic areas are also available.

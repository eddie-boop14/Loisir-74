Tête du Parmelan

Iconic summit of the Parmelan at 1832 m, towering over Annecy with its cliffs. 360° view over Lake Annecy, the Aravis, Mont Blanc, the Bauges and Lake Geneva. Lapiaz plateau, Camille Dunant refuge, paragliding take-off.

About Tête du Parmelan

The Tête du Parmelan is the most iconic summit of the Parmelan plateau, the high point of the cliff overlooking Annecy at 1832 m. It sits on the commune of Dingy-Saint-Clair, at the edge of a vast limestone plateau in the Bornes massif shared between Dingy-Saint-Clair, Fillière and Villaz.

At the summit stand the Camille Dunant refuge — run by the FFCAM (former CAF Annecy) and dating back to 1883 — and a paragliding take-off site. The summit plateau is a striking karst landscape: Urgonian limestone sculpted by runoff into lapiaz, riddled with shafts and caves forming two major underground systems (Bunant, 33.5 km; Diau, over 45 km). From the cross, the panorama opens onto Lake Annecy, the Aravis range, Mont Blanc, the Bauges massif, Le Vuache and, on clear days, Lake Geneva.

What you can do there

Hike from La Blonnière (Dingy-Saint-Clair)

Difficult

12 km loop via the Col du Pertuis and the Grand Montoir, starting at 1009 m, 5h50 walking time on the official HSMB itinerary. Exposed sections equipped with chains.

Hike from Bois Brûlé (Villaz)

Classic route via the Petit and Grand Montoir from the Villaz forest car park. Steep ascent, cabled sections on the Grand Montoir.

Easier access via Anglettaz (Aviernoz / Fillière)

Shortest route from the Chalet de l'Anglettaz (approx. 1500 m). Unpaved track on the last kilometres, careful driving required.

Stay at the Camille Dunant refuge

FFCAM refuge at the summit, simple meals, dorms of 2 to 18. Booking recommended. "Family refuge" label.

Paragliding

Take-off site at the summit, facing Lake Annecy. For autonomous pilots or tandem flights with a professional.

Climbing

Limestone cliffs all around the plateau. Single-pitch and multi-pitch routes, various grades. Major spot of the Annecy area.

Guided caving

The Grotte de l'Enfer, the entrance zone of the Grotte de la Diau and the Grande Glacière are accessible to beginners with a club (CDS74) or a professional (SNPSC).

Stargazing at the refuge

Booking required

Astronomy evenings organised by the Camille Dunant refuge in summer.

How to get there

By car

Three main approaches. From Dingy-Saint-Clair (Thônes valley): D 216 from Annecy, through Dingy village, up to the La Blonnière hamlet, car park at the end of the road at 1009 m. From Villaz: Parmelan forest road from the village, Bois Brûlé car park at 1169 m. From Aviernoz (Fillière): Anglettaz track, unpaved near the end, car park at the Chalet de l'Anglettaz at approx. 1500 m.

When to visit

The period recommended by the tourist office is 15 June to 15 September, subject to favourable snow and weather conditions. A few pointers:

  • Mid-June – early July: snow patches melting out on the Grand and Petit Montoir. Caution on exposed sections.
  • July – August: peak season, refuge open, busy at weekends.
  • September – mid-October: autumn light, lower attendance, refuge open until October.
  • Off-season: winter snowshoeing access for experienced practitioners only. Lapiaz dangerous under snow.

Fog makes the lapiaz plateau dangerous: do not venture on it without visibility.

The Camille Dunant refuge hosts astronomy evenings and Nepalese dinners on booking during the season.

Frequently asked questions

Is access to the Tête du Parmelan paid?
No. Access to the Tête du Parmelan is free and open. Only the Camille Dunant refuge services (meals, overnight) are paid.
Can I bring my dog to the Tête du Parmelan?
Yes. Dogs are allowed at the Tête du Parmelan, strictly on leash due to livestock on the pastures. They are not allowed overnight at the refuge.
When is the best season to climb to the Tête du Parmelan?
The official itinerary to the Tête du Parmelan is recommended from 15 June to 15 September, subject to snow and weather conditions. The Camille Dunant refuge was open from 17 May to 5 October in the 2025 season.
Is the hike to the Tête du Parmelan suitable for children?
The ascent to the Tête du Parmelan via the Grand Montoir includes vertiginous sections equipped with chains, not recommended for young children or people prone to vertigo. The Petit Montoir is less exposed. The Anglettaz route is the most accessible.
Which route should I choose for the Tête du Parmelan?
Three approaches lead to the Tête du Parmelan: La Blonnière (Dingy-Saint-Clair, 12 km loop via Col du Pertuis), Bois Brûlé (Villaz, Grand and Petit Montoir) and the Anglettaz (Aviernoz / Fillière, shorter, unpaved track in approach).
Is there a refuge at the summit of the Tête du Parmelan?
Yes. The Camille Dunant refuge, run by the FFCAM and dating from 1883, sits at 1825 m at the summit of the Tête du Parmelan. Meals, snacks and dorm accommodation (2 to 18 beds). Booking recommended.
Can I paraglide from the Tête du Parmelan?
Yes. The Tête du Parmelan has a paragliding take-off site facing Lake Annecy. For autonomous pilots or tandem flights with a professional only.
Is it dangerous to walk on the plateau of the Tête du Parmelan?
The plateau of the Tête du Parmelan has cracks, shafts and caves hidden by vegetation or snow. Stay on marked trails, avoid the plateau in fog, and supervise children on rocky sections.
Can I reach the Tête du Parmelan via the Perthuis track?
Pedestrian access to the Tête du Parmelan via the Col du Perthuis is free year-round. Vehicle access to the Perthuis track on the southern side is however regulated by a municipal order of Dingy-Saint-Clair.

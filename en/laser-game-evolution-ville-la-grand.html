What is Laser Game Evolution Annemasse

Laser Game Evolution Annemasse is a laser game centre in Ville-la-Grand (74100), right next to Annemasse and only 15 minutes from Geneva. Operated under the national brand Laser Game Evolution, the venue features an indoor arena of several hundred square metres designed over several levels, with immersive blacklight, smoke and sound-effects décor. Each game lasts 15-20 minutes in teams, with latest-generation infrared kit (connected vest and blaster) and a full briefing before each session.

Who is it for? The centre targets the French-Swiss border area: families (usually from age 8), groups of friends, stag/hen parties, business seminars and schools outside school hours. Packages range from a single game to bundles of 2 or 3 consecutive games at reduced per-game rates. Children's birthday parties are a specialty: private room subject to availability, cake and drinks possible, personalised invitations and souvenir photo. The experience is intense but contactless (IR technology) — closed shoes and comfortable clothing recommended.

Practical info and access. Booking is via lasergame-evolution.com or by phone — strongly recommended on weekends, during school holidays and for any group. Prices are around €9-12 per game at full rate, with discounts for multi-game packs and groups. The centre has a waiting area, lockers and drinks. Set in a business park in Ville-la-Grand, car access from Geneva via the Mon-Idée or Moillesulaz border crossings is very quick (15-20 min); from Annecy, allow 45 minutes via the A41.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Where is Laser Game Evolution Annemasse?

The centre is in Ville-la-Grand (74100), right next to Annemasse, 15 minutes from Geneva via the Mon-Idée or Moillesulaz border crossings, and 45 minutes from Annecy on the A41.

From what age can you play?

Laser Game Evolution Annemasse welcomes players from around age 8, provided they are comfortable wearing the vest. For younger players, some centres adapt the equipment. Check with the venue.

How much does a game cost?

A game generally costs €9-12. Bundles of 2 or 3 games at lower per-game rates are available, along with group rates, birthday packages and stag/hen rates. Exact 2026 prices to confirm.

How long is a game?

Each game lasts 15-20 minutes, plus around 10 minutes of briefing before and debriefing after. With a 2-game pack, plan around 1h on site.

Can I pay in Swiss francs?

Main payment is in euros. For CHF or Swiss bank card payment, check directly with the centre. Most international cards are accepted.

Can I organise a birthday party?

Yes, the laser game offers dedicated birthday packages: 2 games + private room (subject to availability) for cake and drinks, personalised invitations and souvenir photo. Book several weeks ahead.

Is there parking on site?

Parking is available on site. Public transport from Annemasse (TAC) or Geneva (TPG via Annemasse) is also an option.

Col de la Colombière

A road pass at 1,613 m between the Aravis range to the south and the Bargy range to the north. The Col de la Colombière links the Borne valley (Le Grand-Bornand, south side) to the Arve valley (Scionzier, Cluses via Le Reposoir, north side). A Tour de France classic with over 20 crossings since 1960, it lies on the Route des Grandes Alpes.

About Col de la Colombière

The Col de la Colombière is a road pass at 1,613 m altitude (the sign reads 1,618 m, the IGN gives 1,607 m), between the Aravis range to the south-south-east and the Bornes massif to the north-north-west. It links the Borne valley, on the south side, to the village of Le Reposoir and the Arve valley, on the north side. The summit lies administratively in the commune of Le Reposoir. The two main approaches start from Le Grand-Bornand (11.7 km, about 6 % average gradient) and from Scionzier via Le Reposoir (16.5 km, about 6.8 % with a final ramp exceeding 10 %).

It is one of the great Tour de France classics, crossed more than 20 times since 1960 and rated Category 1 since 1978. It is part of the Route des Grandes Alpes, which links Thonon-les-Bains to Nice. The summit is a starting point for several walks in the Bargy chain: Lac de Peyre (2,080 m), Pic de Jallouvre (2,408 m), Pointe Balafrasse (2,296 m), Grotte du Montarquis (2,100 m), Tête d'Aufferand (1,983 m). An altitude restaurant, La Colombière, is set up at the summit. The road is closed in winter; it reopens during June depending on snow conditions.

Access

How to get there

By car

South side from Annecy: A41 exit 17 Annecy-Nord, then D909 towards Thônes to Saint-Jean-de-Sixt, then D4 towards Le Grand-Bornand and the pass. North side from Geneva: A40 exit 18 Scionzier, then D4 towards Le Reposoir / Col de la Colombière (9 km).

By public transport

Closest SNCF stations: Cluses on the north side, Annecy on the south side. In summer, free shuttle from the Le Grand-Bornand tourist office between Chinaillon and the pass; check the schedule each year.

By bike

Historic Tour de France pass, ridden on the Route des Grandes Alpes. South side 11.7 km from Le Grand-Bornand, north side 16.5 km from Scionzier via Le Reposoir. Tougher variant via the Col de Romme from Cluses.

When to come

When to visit

The Col de la Colombière is open from June to October, subject to favourable snow conditions:

  • June: the road opens, alpine pastures in flower, moderate traffic. Ideal time for cyclists.
  • July – August: high season, heavy motor traffic (motorbikes, cars). Free shuttle from Chinaillon on weekdays. Early mornings are best for cycling.
  • September – October: lower visitor numbers, autumn light, risk of early closure if there is early snowfall.
  • November to May: road closed to traffic.

The Col de la Colombière features regularly in the Tour de France and Paris-Nice (5th stage 2026 confirmed by the Le Grand-Bornand tourist office). Bearded vulture observation days are organised in season.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is access to the Col de la Colombière paid?

No. Access to the Col de la Colombière is open and free. Parking at the summit is also free.

When is the Col de la Colombière road open?

The Col de la Colombière road is open from June to October, depending on snow conditions. The exact reopening date varies each year. It is closed to traffic from November to May.

Can you bring your dog to the Col de la Colombière?

Yes. Dogs are allowed at the Col de la Colombière. They must be kept on a lead on alpine pasture trails and near herds, which graze on the Bargy slopes in summer.

What is the exact altitude of the Col de la Colombière?

Sources vary. The IGN gives 1,607 m, the road sign at the Col de la Colombière reads 1,618 m, and the Club des Cent Cols and the Route des Grandes Alpes record 1,613 m. The latter figure is the most widely cited.

Which side of the Col de la Colombière is easiest by bike?

The south side of the Col de la Colombière, from Le Grand-Bornand, is clearly easier: 11.7 km at 6 % average gradient. The north side, from Scionzier via Le Reposoir, is 16.5 km with a final ramp exceeding 10 %.

Is there a restaurant at the Col de la Colombière?

Yes. Restaurant La Colombière sits at the summit of the Col de la Colombière, facing the Aravis. It is open in season, while the road is open.

How many times has the Col de la Colombière been in the Tour de France?

The Col de la Colombière has appeared on the Tour de France route since 1960. Sources agree on more than 20 crossings. It was the first Alpine pass crossed by the 2007 Tour de France.

What walks start from the Col de la Colombière?

Several trails leave the Col de la Colombière into the Bargy chain: Lac de Peyre (2,080 m, +467 m), Pic de Jallouvre (2,408 m, +795 m), Pointe Balafrasse (2,296 m, +683 m), Grotte du Montarquis (2,100 m), and, on the opposite side, the Tête d'Aufferand (1,983 m).

Is there a shuttle to the Col de la Colombière without a car?

In summer the Le Grand-Bornand tourist office runs a free shuttle between Chinaillon and the Col de la Colombière. The exact schedule varies each year — check legrandbornand.com before travelling.

Can you ride up the Col de la Colombière on an e-bike?

Yes. An e-bike charging point is at the Hôtel La Croix Saint Maurice in Le Grand-Bornand. The Col de la Colombière can be ridden on an e-bike from both sides.

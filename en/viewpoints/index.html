Viewpoints & passes

Belvederes, passes and summits — where Haute-Savoie looks at itself from above, from Lac d'Annecy to Mont-Blanc.

From the belvedere reached by car to the summit that has to be earned, Haute-Savoie offers panoramas over Lac d'Annecy, Léman and the Mont-Blanc massif. Some are gained on foot, others by cable car.

Read more

Above Annecy, Mont Veyrier and the Semnoz overlook the lake; on the Geneva border, Le Salève is reached by cable car. Higher still, the Aiguille du Midi opens up the high mountains facing Mont-Blanc.

For passes and plateaus, the Plateau de Solaison, the Pont de la Caille and the great passes of the Aravis and the Colombière offer open views, often reachable by car or by bike.

Some viewpoints are gained on foot, others by cable car or by car. Each listing specifies the means of access and the commune.

At altitude, check the weather and the seasonal opening of the passes, closed in winter under the snow.

Frequently asked questions

What are the finest viewpoints over Lac d'Annecy?
Mont Veyrier, the Semnoz and the Col de la Forclaz offer the most famous panoramas over Lac d'Annecy.
How do you reach the viewpoints?
Some are reachable by car or by bike, others are gained on foot or by mechanical lift (cable car, gondola).
When are the passes of Haute-Savoie open?
The high passes (Aravis, Colombière) are generally open from late spring to autumn; they can close in winter because of snow.
Where can you see Mont-Blanc up close?
The Aiguille du Midi at Chamonix and the Brévent offer the most spectacular views of the massif.
Which viewpoint is reachable by car?
The Semnoz, the Col de la Forclaz and the Plateau de Solaison can be reached by car, with parking and an immediate view.

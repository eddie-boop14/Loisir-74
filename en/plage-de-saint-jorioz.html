What is Plage de Saint-Jorioz

Plage de Saint-Jorioz is the only large natural sand beach on Lake Annecy. Located on the west shore facing Dents de Lanfon and Tournette, it spans approximately 2,200 m² and combines fine sand beach (approx. 100 metres long) with vast shaded lawns. The municipal beach is open year-round: free access September to June, paid and lifeguarded access 1 July to 31 August from 9:30 to 17:30.

Certified Blue Flag since 2010, the beach guarantees high environmental standards: daily cleaning during season, sand renewal every four years, waste management and public awareness. It has also earned the Tourism and Disability label thanks to specific facilities: water access ramp and free provision of two amphibious wheelchairs (Tiralo and Hippocampe) in July–August.

The beach is equipped with a 3-level diving board (5 metres high) accessible by swimming from pontoons, a paddling pool and play area for young children, a beach-volleyball court and petanque pitch. A restaurant and snack bar are on site. Immediately nearby: Saint-Jorioz marina, the Reed Path trail, and the Lake Annecy cycle route (9 km to Annecy).

Access

How to get there

By car

From Annecy (9 km, approx. 15 minutes off-season), follow the west shore of Lake Annecy on D909a towards Duingt/Faverges. At Saint-Jorioz, turn left at the traffic light towards the beach (signposted). Large free car park in front of beach entrance. Warning: frequent congestion in summer evenings heading back to Annecy.

Open in Maps

By bike

Lake Annecy cycle route (9 km from Annecy old town, approx. 30 minutes). The route passes a few hundred metres from the beach. Bike racks on site.

When to visit

When to visit

The beach is open year-round but visits vary by season. July–August: high season with lifeguard supervision, paid access, very busy (arrive before 11:00 to find parking). May–June and September: pleasant swimming temperatures (20–25 °C), free beach with fewer visitors, Reed Path in bloom. April and October: cool but swimmable (15–18 °C), ideal for family lakeside walks, minimal crowds. Winter (November–March): free access for walking and enjoying mountain views, swimming for the brave only. Full north exposure guarantees morning-to-evening sunshine in summer as mountains are distant.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How do I book Plage de Saint-Jorioz?

Plage de Saint-Jorioz does not require advance booking. You purchase your entry ticket on site at the kiosks (July–August), or online via the booking system at saint-jorioz.fr (smart card, package or single ticket). Outside July–August, access is free and open.

What is the price for Plage de Saint-Jorioz?

Adult rate (18+): €2.60. Child 10–17 years: €1. Under 10: free. Entry after 16:30: €1 (all ages). Season pass (unlimited access Jul–Aug): €22. Plage de Saint-Jorioz is free September to June. Verified 14 May 2026.

What are the hours for Plage de Saint-Jorioz?

Plage de Saint-Jorioz is open year-round. Free access 1 January to 30 June and 1 September to 31 December. Paid access with lifeguard supervision 1 July to 31 August from 9:30 to 17:30. Closed 7:00 to 9:30 for cleaning in July–August. Verified on official website 14 May 2026 — subject to seasonal changes.

Are dogs allowed at Plage de Saint-Jorioz?

No. Dogs are prohibited at Plage de Saint-Jorioz year-round, even on leash, in accordance with Blue Flag certification.

Is there free parking at Plage de Saint-Jorioz?

Yes. Plage de Saint-Jorioz has a large free shaded car park in front of the entrance. It often fills in summer: arrive before 11:00 or use overflow parking (marina or sports complex).

Is Plage de Saint-Jorioz accessible to people with reduced mobility?

Yes, Plage de Saint-Jorioz is Tourism and Disability certified. A water access ramp and two amphibious wheelchairs (Tiralo and Hippocampe) are provided free in July–August. Contact the lifeguards on duty for details.

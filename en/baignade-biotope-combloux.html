What is Biotope Lake of Combloux

The Biotope Lake of Combloux is France's first public biological swimming site, inaugurated in 2002 and completely renovated in 2022. With a 2,900 m³ pool, the water is naturally filtered by over 10,000 aquatic plants distributed across four interconnected basins, without any chemical treatment. Located in the heart of the village, the site offers a spectacular panoramic view of the Mont Blanc massif, the Aravis range and the Aiguilles de Fiz.

Visitors can swim in four 50-metre lanes, relax on the grassy beach or granite sun deck, and enjoy a family area with gentle sloping access and a sandpit for toddlers. The water temperature, naturally warmed by the sun, varies between 18 and 26°C depending on weather conditions.

The lake stands apart from conventional swimming facilities through its comprehensive ecological approach. Access is limited to 800-1,000 people per day to preserve the balance of the living filtration system. A day wristband allows unlimited in-and-out access. On site: snack bar, parasol and deckchair rental, baby facilities (changing table, food warming), and a motorized lift for wheelchair users. Entry ticket also grants free access to the temporary exhibition at Ferme à Isidore.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is the water chlorinated?

No. The water is biologically filtered by more than 10,000 aquatic plants, without any chemical product. A regeneration-pool system ensures water quality at all times.

What are the opening dates for 2026?

From 13 June to 6 September 2026. High season (4 July-30 August): daily 10:30 am-7 pm. Low season: variable hours, see the official site.

Is booking required?

Booking is strongly recommended in July-August (online at combloux.com/boutique until 10:30 am on the day). Outside school holidays, tickets are available directly on site.

What are the entry prices?

High season: €7.50 adult, €6 child (5-14). Low season: €6.50 adult, €5.50 child. Free for under-5s (ticket required). 12-entry cards and season passes available.

Is the site suitable for young children?

Yes. Family zone with gentle-slope access, sandpit, free for under-5s. Changing tables, possibility to warm up meals, high chairs available. Lifeguard-supervised swimming in season.

Can I leave and return during the day?

Yes. The access wristband lets you leave and return freely throughout the day. It also gives free access to the temporary exhibition at the Ferme à Isidore.

Are there shaded areas?

Few naturally shaded areas. Parasol rental available on site (€6, limited stock). 80 dedicated spots on the grass beach.

Can we picnic on site?

Yes, picnicking on a towel is allowed. Snack bar on site and Restaurant Alexperience with a terrace facing the lake.

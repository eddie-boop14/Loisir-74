What is The Peasant Museum, a PAYSALP Culture & Heritage site

The Musée Paysan at Viuz-en-Sallaz is one of the flagship sites of PAYSALP Culture & Patrimoine, a recognised association running a fully fledged multi-site ecomuseum in the Haut-Faucigny. The museum offers an immersive 500 m² exhibition that retraces mountain life between 1850 and 1950, a pivotal period between ancestral traditions and early modernisation.

The route brings to life the craftsmen, peasants and villagers of the time: carpenter, blacksmith, farmers, schoolteacher. Each tells their daily story through careful reconstructions, authentic objects and audio or tactile devices. A 1924 schoolroom is built into the visit: visitors can sit on the wooden benches and try a few questions from the old Certificat d'Études Primaires — a moment always enjoyed by families and school groups alike.

The museum also offers two paid bookable activities: the "Absurd Game" (life-size board game, €10 adult / €7 child) and the "Explor Games" (immersive investigation game, €10/person). Museum prices: €7 adult, €5 child 5-15, free under 5. Hours: Monday-Friday 10 am-12.30 pm and 1.30 pm-5 pm (until 6 pm in summer from 10 July to 1 September). Group activities year-round by reservation. Address: 628 avenue de Savoie, 74250 Viuz-en-Sallaz.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How much does the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz cost?

Prices for the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz: €7 adult, €5 child 5-15, free under 5. Absurd Game €10 adult / €7 child. Explor Games €10/person. Verified 10 June 2026.

What are the opening hours of the PAYSALP Peasant Museum?

The PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz is open Monday to Friday 10 am-12.30 pm and 1.30 pm-5 pm (until 6 pm from 10 July to 1 September). Verified 10 June 2026.

What is there to see at the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz?

The PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz offers a 500 m² immersive exhibition on mountain life 1850-1950, with a 1924 classroom where you can sit a certificat d'études.

Is the PAYSALP Peasant Museum suitable for children?

Yes, perfectly. The PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz features an immersive scenography that children love, an interactive 1924 classroom and Absurd Game / Explor Games activities. Free under 5.

Where is the PAYSALP Peasant Museum?

The PAYSALP Peasant Museum is at 628 avenue de Savoie, 74250 Viuz-en-Sallaz, 25 min from Annemasse (D907) and 25 min from Cluses (D26). Free on-site parking.

Is the PAYSALP Peasant Museum accessible to visitors with reduced mobility?

Yes. The building of the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz is accessible to people with reduced mobility.

What is the PAYSALP Peasant Museum's Absurd Game?

The PAYSALP Peasant Museum's Absurd Game is a life-size board game played inside the museum (€10 adult, €7 child, by reservation). A fun activity for families and groups.

How long should I plan for the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz?

Allow 1 h 30 to 2 h to visit the PAYSALP Peasant Museum at Viuz-en-Sallaz, depending on engagement with the 1924 classroom and possible participation in the Absurd Game or Explor Games.

Outdoor thrills

Flight, whitewater, cliffs and big thrills — the mountains in adrenaline mode.

For thrill-seekers, Haute-Savoie is a full-scale playground: free flight facing Mont-Blanc, whitewater, treetop courses and gorge descents.

Read more

In the air, paragliding takes off from the Forclaz above Annecy and from the Arve valley; on the ground, via ferrata, canyoning and rafting bring torrents and cliffs alive.

More family-friendly, the treetop adventure parks and, in winter, dog sledding outings round out the picture.

Many of these activities go through a provider (school, guides' bureau). Each listing links to the official site for conditions and booking.

There are beginner formats accessible to everyone: tandem flights, easy via ferrata, children's treetop courses.

Frequently asked questions

Where to paraglide in Haute-Savoie?
The Forclaz site above Lac d'Annecy is one of the most renowned in the world; the Arve valley also offers many take-offs facing Mont-Blanc.
Are canyoning and rafting supervised?
Yes, these activities are run with a guide or an approved base, which provides the equipment and adapts the route to your level.
Are treetop courses suitable for children?
Yes: parks offer courses by level and height, from a young age, with a continuous lifeline.
Do you need to be sporty?
Not necessarily: introductory formats (tandem flight, easy via ferrata, children's treetop course) are accessible to all.
Where to go dog sledding?
In high-altitude resorts in winter (Grand-Bornand, Aravis, Arve valley), mushers offer first rides and outings with a team.
